Hole Card

At the start of each lot, you're dealt one private position — fully revealed (market, side, entry, price, size). Only you can see it. This is your information edge.

Shared Positions

One position flips for everyone on Street 2, another on Street 3. Combined with your hole card, you see more of the lot's true value as streets progress.

Burn Cards

At least one position stays hidden until the reveal. Nobody sees it. This makes every lot unsolvable — you're always pricing under uncertainty.

Payout Range

A deliberately wide bracket (2-3x wider than needed). The true value falls somewhere inside. Your job: narrow it down with the signals you have.

TWO GAMES IN ONE

The fun is adversarial price discovery, not gambling.

GAME 1

Price the Lot

Read the signals. Use your hole card. Watch positions flip on each street. Estimate the true value. The more you understand prediction markets, the sharper your edge.

GAME 2

Price the Players

All bids are revealed between streets. Who overbid on Street 1? Who's holding back? Who has a strong hole card? The real long-term skill is reading betting patterns, not just reading the lot.
